BIO

Hello!

I started my PhD in New Orleans at Tulane University and now I'm finishing up my PhD in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at the University of Tennessee Knoxville.

My academic interests began in sustainable agriculture and land use change's impacts on conservation. While working abroad in Canada after graduation from Furman University in 2013, I decided to pursue experiences in community engagement. Thus, I chose to work in Corporant, Haiti for a year at Partners in Agriculture sustainable agriculture school. Afterwards, I worked with sustainable gardeners in South Carolina, partnering with SC Parks and Recreation to carry out historical landscape research on Redcliffe Plantation State Historic Site.

After all this I decided to engage in academia - I found I loved teaching in Haiti and wanted to pursue community engaged research as a university professor. I joined the Blum lab at Tulane University in 2015 working on aquatic invasive species impact on native fauna. After a year I developed a fascination with eco- and rapid evolution and have since begun a project pursuing these interests.

My current work examines ecological resilience in the face of climate change using a soil-stored seed bank of Olney's bulrush, a brackish marsh sedge, and its capacity to adapt rapidly to salinity stress resulting from sea level rise. I work in Kirkpatrick Marsh at the Smithsonian Environmental Research Center in Edgewater, Maryland. This work is vitally important: understanding the rate of adaptation of this foundational sedge can help us project the futures of coastlines as climate change worsens. By resurrecting seeds through what has become known as 'resurrection ecology', I am able to compare young and old populations of S. americanus in common conditions. I use experiments combined with whole genome sequencing, RADSeq, and RNASeq to dissect the genetic underpinnings of adaptation.

Mike Blum and his wife, Liz Derryberry, took positions at the University of Tennessee - Knoxville and I joined my lab in moving to Knoxville in August 2017.
